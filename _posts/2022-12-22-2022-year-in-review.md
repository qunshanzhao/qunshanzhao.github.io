Today is the last working day of the year — tomorrow I set off for a holiday in Morocco! Hard to believe this is my first trip abroad since coming to the UK. Aside from being too lazy to sort out visas, the rest of the blame lies entirely with being stranded on this island by the pandemic.

I just re-read last year's summary and found that the wishes basically all came true — except that I still have not made it to continental Europe. This year I attended five conferences within the UK: GISRUK 2022 (Liverpool), IMGS 2022 (Edinburgh), ABCP 2022 (Birmingham), RGS 2022 (Newcastle), and CATE 2022 (Edinburgh). IMGS and CATE both happened to be in Edinburgh — one upside of a small country is that joining the fun is easy. Counting students and postdocs, we gave six conference presentations in total, and I gave five invited academic talks myself. Next year I am involved in organising three major academic conferences: GISRUK 2023 in Glasgow, CPGIS 2023 in London, and GIScience 2023 in Leeds. If you have the time, the funding, and the visa, do come and have a look around the UK.

Publications came out slightly ahead of last year: 4 journal papers published, 4 under review, and 3 conference papers. There is still a backlog of papers sitting inside projects waiting to be revised and submitted — that will have to wait for next year. On the funding side it was a rather successful year: two ESRC-funded PhD studentship projects (one as PI, one as Co-I), a £300k SDAI grant (with a postdoc collaborator at the centre as PI), a £1.6m Gates Foundation project in Africa (Co-I), a 3D city modelling project for Glasgow City Council (PI), and two small teaching-focused grants supporting the Urban Analytics MSc (PI). Right at the end of the year an AGILE funding initiative grant (PI) landed as well, which lets us continue the spopt open-source development following this year's GSoC project. Together with the existing UBDC projects, that adds up to quite a lot — the question now is how to turn it all into academic output and societal impact.

We advertised jobs all year long — three or four rounds of postdoc and faculty recruitment — so there will be plenty of new faces joining us next year. There are still 2 postdoc and 3 faculty positions open, plus a fully funded PhD studentship on African urban remote sensing (related to the IDEAMAPS theme) opening early next year.

On teaching: in spring 2022, with some international students still unable to come to the UK, I taught with pre-recorded videos plus a simultaneous in-person and Zoom livestream. By this autumn teaching had basically returned to pre-pandemic normality — it took almost three years to get back to normal.

Beyond my regular work at Glasgow, I took on some external roles this year: joining an ESRC grant assessment panel, serving as the final-year external examiner for Warwick's Urban Analytics MSc, and sitting on the ESRC Future Data Services programme advisory board. The more I deal with funders, the more novel their internal workings seem — full of resource competition and negotiated compromise. The ESRC grant assessment panel was a particularly useful experience: you get to see how a research proposal passes through layer after layer of screening to the moment it finally wins funding. There are many uncontrollable factors along the way. My advice: polish your research ideas and your proposal properly, take every stage seriously (especially responses to reviewer comments), and after that — wish for luck. (One day when I have time I should write a piece specifically about the UK grant assessment process; it really is interesting.)

Finally, I should mention that I was promoted to Senior Lecturer (Associate Professor) this year. My thanks to the leadership and colleagues at Glasgow for their support and encouragement, and to the PhD students and postdocs I work with — onwards to the next goal.

On other fronts: I hope mainland China comes through this wave of the pandemic smoothly; once quarantine policies relax and flights recover next year, I plan to go back to China for a visit (I last left China in January 2016). The war in Ukraine shows no sign of stopping, European energy prices are sky-high, and with the Fed, the Bank of England, and the ECB raising rates relentlessly, a global recession is looming. I wish everyone good health and stable work — treasure the time with family and friends. Here's to meeting more new friends next year, and to reunions with old ones!
