Just back from skiing at Glencoe this afternoon — a good moment to fill the hole I dug at the start of the holidays.

2021 was, on the whole, a bewildering year: the pandemic raged from start to finish. In the first two months, the UK's situation was so severe that nurseries closed, so I looked after the little one by day and started recording lectures after ten at night. Teaching programming entirely online is astonishingly time-consuming — a 3-hour tutorial could come with over an hour of extra Q&A. In the second half of the year, hybrid teaching became a serious test of classroom equipment: I ended up speaking into the microphones of the classroom desktop and my laptop simultaneously, both logged into Zoom sharing screens, while students in the room ideally answered questions into a phone logged into Zoom as well, so that online students could hear them. Even now I do not know how next semester will be taught. From a teaching perspective, though, the pandemic has been a catalyst — especially for a slow-moving system like the UK's. ASU pushed fully online teaching hard years ago, and in hindsight it really was ahead of the curve. Good lecture material is now everywhere online; designing better small-group, discussion-based teaching experiences will be what distinguishes the quality of university education in the future. University was never just about sitting through lectures and collecting a diploma — the mutual learning and conversation with teachers and classmates often brings the most unexpected rewards.

The pandemic was not entirely without upside, especially for those of us in work-from-home-able fields. Governments everywhere printed money, the UK included — and after handing money to people and showering it on companies, a little was left over for research. In November 2020 the centre won an ESRC "quick spending" grant (grandly titled *World Class Research Infrastructure*: £213m to upgrade the UK's research infrastructure, with UBDC receiving a further award from UKRI-ESRC) — to be spent by the end of March 2021. So from November onwards we scrambled to advertise and recruit. The original proposal had planned a travel survey, which COVID made impossible, so we amended it and bought mobile phone app data for the Greater Glasgow area — and the whole project became a full year of mobile phone app research in 2021. The results were excellent. First, this is a genuinely hot and practically meaningful direction: the centre has collaborated with Glasgow City Council for five or six years, and when they heard we had bought this data, they funded us to do more than half a year of government work on top — using app data to understand movement patterns during the pandemic: home location estimation, building OD matrices, and computing place visitation patterns. Second, on the data side, our collaborations with the vendors (Huq and Tamoco) went well enough that the centre decided to purchase their data covering the past 3 and coming 2 years for the whole UK, to be shared under conditions with the community for non-profit academic research. Third, in a very short time we hired one excellent postdoc and extended another postdoc's contract, laying foundations for the research ahead. Doing research with novel data really is a blue ocean. Academia used to lack data, or could not afford high-quality data; companies lack the time, resources, and expertise to do socially useful but unprofitable analysis. Buying corporate data with public research funds and distributing it to academia saves enormous amounts of one-to-one contract negotiation, simplifies the process, and raises efficiency all round.

News about the centre's mobility research: *How people move around cities matters*.

Beyond the new-forms-of-data research at the centre, a colleague and I ran a project on indoor environmental quality (*Building capacity for sensor-enhanced surveys*). It is a small project, but because it involves not only the social survey component but also a lot of hardware and software sensor integration, it has been great fun. Beyond that, I remain deeply interested in high-resolution thermal remote sensing, 3D cities, spatial location optimisation, and applied machine learning. In the summer, together with good friends at ASU, I organised a round of Google Summer of Code — a Brazilian student helped us write some fundamental spatial location optimisation algorithms into PySAL, with the paper under review at JOSS. I hope we can run another round next summer and further improve the reproducibility and openness of spatial optimisation methods: *A summer of Python coding — open-source facility location modelling (spopt) development*.

This year I joined the editorial board of Springer's [Urban Book Series](https://www.springer.com/series/14773/editors). One perk is a free copy of every new book in the series. Urban science is so broad a field that I have yet to receive a book I could really sink into — or perhaps the discipline still trusts journals more, and book chapters always feel a touch less rigorous. In the second half of the year I joined the committee of the RGS-IBG Quantitative Methods Research Group, hoping to keep advancing quantitative methods in geography and urban science. Having served three years with the AAG's Spatial Analysis and Modeling (SAM) group, I hope to foster some UK–US collaborative research next year.

Before year's end, the centre won its next 2.5-year extension (*Urban Big Data Centre's data service awarded new funding from ESRC*) — £4 million in total. From January 2022 the centre is hiring: two data scientists, one postdoc in urban remote sensing and sensing (the ad goes out in January — this one is mine, enquiries welcome), and a part-time project officer. Beyond those, a new PhD student will be recruited around March, working with the Salvation Army to analyse their Employment Plus programme, and four or five proposals are still awaiting final outcomes — if additional PhD or postdoc positions come through, more ads will follow.

Finally, papers. This year I published 4 journal articles, 1 book chapter, and 1 GIScience conference paper. The book chapter and one journal paper came out of first-year MSc student projects; the conference paper was a quick synthesis of this year's phone app data research; two more journal papers were collaborations on optimisation and urban heat islands. My only first-authored piece wrapped up research from my 2018 postdoc days — finishing what I started. Citations saw no particular breakthrough — steady and unspectacular as ever. My pursuit in research remains the combination of long-term intellectual interest (fascinating, though often without direct use) and real societal value (useful — especially in today's social sciences); who knows when a hot topic might land on your head. All of this, of course, rests on the academic freedom afforded by the UK's permanent contracts from assistant-professor level onwards — chasing tenure would be a different game entirely. Three or four papers are under review, plus two deep-learning application papers with last year's MSc students, three or four mobile-app and housing-sensor papers with the centre's postdocs, and a queue of proposals that naturally need writing — next year will be full of writing tasks.

New Year's wish: may the pandemic dissipate, so that in 2022 I can attend an in-person conference and finally visit continental Europe. Thanks to all colleagues, friends, teachers, and collaborators for their help and support, and to my family for their constant company. Since I came to the UK, three of my relatives have passed away in the past two and a half years, and in 2019 I also lost a close childhood friend; I myself have not been back to China for six years. So my one wish is that everyone stays healthy, safe, and happy — until we meet again.

## 中文原文

今天下午刚从 Glencoe 滑雪回来,就顺路把假期开始的时候挖的坑填了吧。。

2021年整体来说就很迷,疫情整整闹了一年。头两个月英国疫情太严重把幼儿园关了,一边带娃一边晚上十点多开始录课,全在线教编程真是太消耗时间了,3 个小时的 tutorial 还能额外答疑超过 1 小时。到了下半年开始hybrid教学也很考验教室的设备水平,最后是同时对着教室台式电脑还有笔记本电脑的麦克风说话,两台电脑同时登zoom分享屏幕,然后教室里的同学回答问题最好还要用手机登一个 zoom 对着说,让线上的同学也能听见。一直到现在也还不知道下学期到底要怎么上课。我觉得从教学的角度来说,疫情也是一个催化剂,尤其是对于英国这种变化缓慢的系统。以前ASU就大力推进全在线教学,现在看来确实是走在了前面。现在好的lecture材料是全网都是,如何更好的设计小班讨论教学体验,会是区分未来大学教育质量的关键。毕竟大学并不是上上课发个文凭,上学过程中和老师同学的相互学习和沟通,往往能带来很多不一样的收获。

不过疫情也不是完全没有好处,尤其对于我们可以 work from home 的专业。因为疫情各国政府都印了很多钱,英国也不例外,除了给人民发钱,给企业撒钱,总是会剩下一点给科研。2020年11月中心拿到了ESRC Quick Spending的项目 (美其名曰 World Class Research Infrastructure: £213m to upgrade the UK's world-class research infrastructure and Urban Big Data Centre receives further award from UKRI-ESRC),2021 年三月底就要把钱花完。于是从11月开始就和同事一起各种忙着打广告和招人。因为之前proposal是需要做 travel survey,但是covid做不了,就临时修改了 proposal 的内容,买了格拉斯哥大地区的mobile phone app数据,最后整个项目变成做了2021 年一整年的mobile phone app研究。从结果上来说是很好的,一是这是一个非常热门而且有实际意义的方向。中心和格拉斯哥市政府在过去的五六年有长期的合作,这次他们听说我们买了这个数据,就连带给我们钱帮他们做了半年多的政府项目。项目内容主要是利用手机 app 数据来理解人们在疫情期间的出行模式,比如 home location estimation,create OD matrix,和计算一些 place visitation pattern。第二是数据方面,因为和数据服务商(Huq Data and Tamoco Data)的合作都还算愉快,各方的项目需求也很多,于是中心打算继续购买他们过去 3 年和未来 2 年全英国的数据,之后有条件的分享给大家做非营利性的学术研究。第三是在非常短的时间内雇佣了 1 位非常优秀的博后,同时延长了另一位博后的合同,为接下来的研究奠定了基础。用新型数据做科研真是个蓝海,能做的东西不少。以前学术界没有数据或者买不起得不到高质量数据,公司没有时间,资源,和知识来做一些对社会有用但是不赚钱的数据分析,现在通过政府科研项目资金购买公司数据,分发给学术界做研究,既节约了大量的一对一的合同谈判时间,也简化了流程,提高了效率。

关于中心在 mobility 研究方面的新闻:How people move around cities matters

除了在中心做关于新型数据的研究,我和同事还在做一个关注于室内环境质量的项目(Building capacity for sensor-enhanced surveys)。虽然是一个小项目,但是因为项目里面不仅仅涉及到社会调查的部分,还会涉及到到很多硬件和软件相关的传感器集成,总体来说还是挺好玩的。除此之外,我个人还是一直对高分辨率热遥感,三维城市,空间区位优化,还有机器学习的应用非常感兴趣。夏天的时候和 ASU 的好友一起组织了一期Google Summer of Code,巴西小哥帮我们把一些基本的空间区位优化的算法写到了pysal里面,文章还在joss审稿。希望明年夏天能一起再做一期,进一步提升空间优化方法的可重复性和开放性。A summer of python coding - open-source facility location modelling (Spopt) development

今年加入了springer的urban book series的editorial board (https://www.springer.com/series/14773/editors)。一个好处就是可以拿到这个系列里之后所有新书的free copy。感觉城市科学方面的研究实在太广了,我到现在还没收到一本能好好看的下去的书,还有可能就是行业内还是更认学术期刊,总觉得 book chapter 少了点严谨。今年下半年加入了英国地理学家协会 (RGS-IBG) 的Quantitative Methods Research Group (Committee),还是希望能继续推动定量方法在地理学和城市科学里的应用。因为之前在 AAG 的 SAM 服务过 3 年,希望明年能推动一些英美之间的合作研究。

今年年底之前中心中了接下来2年半的延长项目(Urban Big Data Centre's data service awarded new funding from ESRC),总共四百万英镑。从2022 年1月份开始中心有一系列的招聘,包括两名数据科学家 (Data Scientist (Digital Storytelling Engineer) at University of Glasgow and Data Scientist at University of Glasgow),一名城市遥感和传感方面的博后(广告一月份会发出来,主要由我负责,欢迎大家垂询),还有一名兼职的项目管理人员(Project Officer at University of Glasgow),如果有合适的欢迎大家申请和咨询。除此之外,明年目前确定的是在三月份左右会招一名新的博士生,是和英国的 Charity Salvation Army 合作一起分析他们的 employment+ program。除了这些已经确定的招聘机会以外,还有四五个proposal在等待最终的结果,如果有额外的博士或者博士后名额,会继续发广告招聘。

最后谈谈今年的文章。今年总共发表了 4 篇 journal articles,1 篇 book chapter,1 篇GIScience 的会议论文。其中 book chapter 和一篇期刊是出自第一年MSc学生的项目,会议是今年手机app数据研究的一个简单汇总,还有另外2篇期刊是关于优化和城市热岛的合作文章。唯一的一篇一作是把 2018 年博后期间做的研究收尾了,也算是有始有终。引用方面没有什么特别的突破,还是老样子不温不火。一直以来对于科研的追求还是在于长期的科研兴趣(有趣但是往往没有什么直接的用处)和实际的社会价值(有用,尤其在现在的社科领域),谁知道哪天热点就落自己头上了呢。当然这些都是基于英国从助理教授就开始的长期合同制度带来的某种程度上的学术自由吧,如果要评 tenured 自然是有另外一种玩法。现在在审的文章有个 3-4 篇,加上和去年的 MSc 同学一起做的 2 篇深度学习应用的文章,和中心博后一起做的mobile phone app 和 housing sensor enhance survey 的 3-4 篇文章,还有一些顺理成章要写的 proposal,明年应该还是会有很多写作任务。

新年愿望,就希望疫情能消散,让我能在 2022 年开次线下的会议,再去一次欧陆吧。感谢所有同事朋友老师合作者的帮助和支持,还有家人一直以来的支持和陪伴。在我来英国以后,过去的 2 年半里我有三位亲人离开了人世,19 年还有一位从小非常要好的同学也不在了,我自己也有 6 年时间没有回中国。所以唯一的愿望就是希望大家都能身体健康,平安快乐,期待下一次的相会。
