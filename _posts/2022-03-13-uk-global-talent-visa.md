Recently, while helping our centre recruit a new postdoc, I had to look into UK work visas for researchers. After exchanging notes with the university's HR, I realised that this visa — updated from the old Tier 1 — is a major boon for recruiting talent into UK academia. Details are on the UK government website: [Apply for the Global Talent visa](https://www.gov.uk/global-talent) (I must say the UK government's visa pages are done remarkably carefully and thoughtfully).

The visa involves two steps: first obtaining an **endorsement** — essentially certification that you are a talent — and second, the visa application process itself.

There are four endorsement routes: (1) holding a faculty appointment at associate professor level or above, or a senior research position at an institution; (2) holding one of a list of hard-to-win individual research fellowships; (3) holding a postdoctoral position funded by the UK government or another endorsed funder; and (4) peer review. Of the four, the third is comparatively the easiest: if the applicant has a PhD, the grant runs for more than 2 years in total, and the total award exceeds £30,000, you can apply for the endorsement directly.

With an endorsement via the first three routes, you can apply for UK permanent residency (settlement) after three years. Via the fourth route it may take 3 or 5 years. Overall this is extremely good news for people hoping to build a stable academic research career in the UK, especially in less "hot" fields. The policy exists in part because, post-Brexit, the UK wants to attract more high-skilled talent to replace those who used to come freely from the continent — the reinstated PSW visa (two years of post-study work rights for UK graduates) is a similar measure.

Compared with the ordinary Tier 2 work visa, the Global Talent Visa has many advantages. Tier 2 can only be issued for 3 or 5 years; the Global Talent Visa can be as short as one year or as long as five. Tier 2 requires proof of English, and sensitive subjects need ATAS clearance; the Global Talent Visa requires neither, so applications are processed faster. Global Talent Visa holders can also be self-employed or take on other work, as long as it relates to their field.

That was my quick explanation in plain terms. Below are some specifics in English for reference. Because the Global Talent Visa can count time spent on certain other visa types towards settlement, someone nearing three years on a Tier 2 visa who meets the Global Talent criteria can switch to a one-year Global Talent Visa and apply for settlement immediately, rather than waiting out the full five years. For now the visa does not look hard to obtain; whether the UK government will tighten the policy over time — gradually raising the requirements to control numbers — remains to be seen.

- **Academic and research appointments (fast-track)** — endorsement for individuals who have accepted a job in which they will have responsibility for academic, research or innovation leadership, or are an emerging leader. This includes but is not limited to Professor, Associate Professor and Senior Group Leader appointments. The appointment must be at an approved UK higher education institution or research institute, and certain recruitment requirements must be met. This route is administered by the British Academy, the Royal Academy of Engineering, and the Royal Society.
- **Individual fellowships (fast-track)** — endorsement for individuals who have been awarded an individual fellowship on the list approved by the British Academy, the Royal Academy of Engineering, and the Royal Society. The fellowship must be held currently or within the last 12 months.
- **Endorsed funders (fast-track)** — endorsement for researchers and specialists, employed or hosted by a department, faculty or college, whose name or job title is specified in a successful grant application from an endorsed funder approved by UKRI.
- **Peer-reviewed application (standard)** — endorsement for individuals who meet all eligibility criteria and demonstrate that they are a proven leader in their field (Exceptional Talent) or have the potential to become one (Exceptional Promise). Applications are assessed by fellows of the British Academy, the Royal Academy of Engineering, or the Royal Society.

The most utilised route is the **Endorsed Funder** route. Its eligibility criteria are:

- You have a grant award provided by an Endorsed Funder as shown in the Immigration Rules;
- Your grant award is worth £30,000 or more (note: this is the total amount, not per annum or a salary equivalent);
- Your grant award has a minimum duration of 24 months, including the time left on the contract;
- Your grant award names you as the applicant under directly incurred costs.
