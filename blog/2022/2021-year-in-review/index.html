2021 Year in Review: The Pandemic, Research Interests, and Life

Created on January 01, 2022

2022 В  В· В  year-in-review В  academia В  covid-19

Translated from my original Chinese post on Zhihu (published January 2022). дёӯж–ҮеҺҹж–Үи§Ғжң¬йЎөдёӢж–№ вҖ” the full Chinese original follows the English text.

Just back from skiing at Glencoe this afternoon вҖ” a good moment to fill the hole I dug at the start of the holidays.

2021 was, on the whole, a bewildering year: the pandemic raged from start to finish. In the first two months, the UKвҖҷs situation was so severe that nurseries closed, so I looked after the little one by day and started recording lectures after ten at night. Teaching programming entirely online is astonishingly time-consuming вҖ” a 3-hour tutorial could come with over an hour of extra Q&A. In the second half of the year, hybrid teaching became a serious test of classroom equipment: I ended up speaking into the microphones of the classroom desktop and my laptop simultaneously, both logged into Zoom sharing screens, while students in the room ideally answered questions into a phone logged into Zoom as well, so that online students could hear them. Even now I do not know how next semester will be taught. From a teaching perspective, though, the pandemic has been a catalyst вҖ” especially for a slow-moving system like the UKвҖҷs. ASU pushed fully online teaching hard years ago, and in hindsight it really was ahead of the curve. Good lecture material is now everywhere online; designing better small-group, discussion-based teaching experiences will be what distinguishes the quality of university education in the future. University was never just about sitting through lectures and collecting a diploma вҖ” the mutual learning and conversation with teachers and classmates often brings the most unexpected rewards.

The pandemic was not entirely without upside, especially for those of us in work-from-home-able fields. Governments everywhere printed money, the UK included вҖ” and after handing money to people and showering it on companies, a little was left over for research. In November 2020 the centre won an ESRC вҖңquick spendingвҖқ grant (grandly titled World Class Research Infrastructure: ВЈ213m to upgrade the UKвҖҷs research infrastructure, with UBDC receiving a further award from UKRI-ESRC) вҖ” to be spent by the end of March 2021. So from November onwards we scrambled to advertise and recruit. The original proposal had planned a travel survey, which COVID made impossible, so we amended it and bought mobile phone app data for the Greater Glasgow area вҖ” and the whole project became a full year of mobile phone app research in 2021. The results were excellent. First, this is a genuinely hot and practically meaningful direction: the centre has collaborated with Glasgow City Council for five or six years, and when they heard we had bought this data, they funded us to do more than half a year of government work on top вҖ” using app data to understand movement patterns during the pandemic: home location estimation, building OD matrices, and computing place visitation patterns. Second, on the data side, our collaborations with the vendors (Huq and Tamoco) went well enough that the centre decided to purchase their data covering the past 3 and coming 2 years for the whole UK, to be shared under conditions with the community for non-profit academic research. Third, in a very short time we hired one excellent postdoc and extended another postdocвҖҷs contract, laying foundations for the research ahead. Doing research with novel data really is a blue ocean. Academia used to lack data, or could not afford high-quality data; companies lack the time, resources, and expertise to do socially useful but unprofitable analysis. Buying corporate data with public research funds and distributing it to academia saves enormous amounts of one-to-one contract negotiation, simplifies the process, and raises efficiency all round.

News about the centreвҖҷs mobility research: How people move around cities matters.

Beyond the new-forms-of-data research at the centre, a colleague and I ran a project on indoor environmental quality (Building capacity for sensor-enhanced surveys). It is a small project, but because it involves not only the social survey component but also a lot of hardware and software sensor integration, it has been great fun. Beyond that, I remain deeply interested in high-resolution thermal remote sensing, 3D cities, spatial location optimisation, and applied machine learning. In the summer, together with good friends at ASU, I organised a round of Google Summer of Code вҖ” a Brazilian student helped us write some fundamental spatial location optimisation algorithms into PySAL, with the paper under review at JOSS. I hope we can run another round next summer and further improve the reproducibility and openness of spatial optimisation methods: A summer of Python coding вҖ” open-source facility location modelling (spopt) development.

This year I joined the editorial board of SpringerвҖҷs Urban Book Series. One perk is a free copy of every new book in the series. Urban science is so broad a field that I have yet to receive a book I could really sink into вҖ” or perhaps the discipline still trusts journals more, and book chapters always feel a touch less rigorous. In the second half of the year I joined the committee of the RGS-IBG Quantitative Methods Research Group, hoping to keep advancing quantitative methods in geography and urban science. Having served three years with the AAGвҖҷs Spatial Analysis and Modeling (SAM) group, I hope to foster some UKвҖ“US collaborative research next year.

Before yearвҖҷs end, the centre won its next 2.5-year extension (Urban Big Data CentreвҖҷs data service awarded new funding from ESRC) вҖ” ВЈ4 million in total. From January 2022 the centre is hiring: two data scientists, one postdoc in urban remote sensing and sensing (the ad goes out in January вҖ” this one is mine, enquiries welcome), and a part-time project officer. Beyond those, a new PhD student will be recruited around March, working with the Salvation Army to analyse their Employment Plus programme, and four or five proposals are still awaiting final outcomes вҖ” if additional PhD or postdoc positions come through, more ads will follow.

Finally, papers. This year I published 4 journal articles, 1 book chapter, and 1 GIScience conference paper. The book chapter and one journal paper came out of first-year MSc student projects; the conference paper was a quick synthesis of this yearвҖҷs phone app data research; two more journal papers were collaborations on optimisation and urban heat islands. My only first-authored piece wrapped up research from my 2018 postdoc days вҖ” finishing what I started. Citations saw no particular breakthrough вҖ” steady and unspectacular as ever. My pursuit in research remains the combination of long-term intellectual interest (fascinating, though often without direct use) and real societal value (useful вҖ” especially in todayвҖҷs social sciences); who knows when a hot topic might land on your head. All of this, of course, rests on the academic freedom afforded by the UKвҖҷs permanent contracts from assistant-professor level onwards вҖ” chasing tenure would be a different game entirely. Three or four papers are under review, plus two deep-learning application papers with last yearвҖҷs MSc students, three or four mobile-app and housing-sensor papers with the centreвҖҷs postdocs, and a queue of proposals that naturally need writing вҖ” next year will be full of writing tasks.

New YearвҖҷs wish: may the pandemic dissipate, so that in 2022 I can attend an in-person conference and finally visit continental Europe. Thanks to all colleagues, friends, teachers, and collaborators for their help and support, and to my family for their constant company. Since I came to the UK, three of my relatives have passed away in the past two and a half years, and in 2019 I also lost a close childhood friend; I myself have not been back to China for six years. So my one wish is that everyone stays healthy, safe, and happy вҖ” until we meet again.

д»Ҡе№ҙе№ҙеә•д№ӢеүҚдёӯеҝғдёӯдәҶжҺҘдёӢжқҘ2е№ҙеҚҠзҡ„е»¶й•ҝйЎ№зӣ®(Urban Big Data CentreвҖҷs data service awarded new funding from ESRC),жҖ»е…ұеӣӣзҷҫдёҮиӢұй•‘гҖӮд»Һ2022 е№ҙ1жңҲд»ҪејҖе§ӢдёӯеҝғжңүдёҖзі»еҲ—зҡ„жӢӣиҒҳ,еҢ…жӢ¬дёӨеҗҚж•°жҚ®з§‘еӯҰе®¶ (Data Scientist (Digital Storytelling Engineer) at University of Glasgow and Data Scientist at University of Glasgow),дёҖеҗҚеҹҺеёӮйҒҘж„ҹе’Ңдј ж„ҹж–№йқўзҡ„еҚҡеҗҺ(е№ҝе‘ҠдёҖжңҲд»ҪдјҡеҸ‘еҮәжқҘ,дё»иҰҒз”ұжҲ‘иҙҹиҙЈ,ж¬ўиҝҺеӨ§е®¶еһӮиҜў),иҝҳжңүдёҖеҗҚе…јиҒҢзҡ„йЎ№зӣ®з®ЎзҗҶдәәе‘ҳ(Project Officer at University of Glasgow),еҰӮжһңжңүеҗҲйҖӮзҡ„ж¬ўиҝҺеӨ§е®¶з”іиҜ·е’Ңе’ЁиҜўгҖӮйҷӨжӯӨд№ӢеӨ–,жҳҺе№ҙзӣ®еүҚзЎ®е®ҡзҡ„жҳҜеңЁдёүжңҲд»Ҫе·ҰеҸідјҡжӢӣдёҖеҗҚж–°зҡ„еҚҡеЈ«з”ҹ,жҳҜе’ҢиӢұеӣҪзҡ„ Charity Salvation Army еҗҲдҪңдёҖиө·еҲҶжһҗд»–д»¬зҡ„ employment+ programгҖӮйҷӨдәҶиҝҷдәӣе·Із»ҸзЎ®е®ҡзҡ„жӢӣиҒҳжңәдјҡд»ҘеӨ–,иҝҳжңүеӣӣдә”дёӘproposalеңЁзӯүеҫ…жңҖз»Ҳзҡ„з»“жһң,еҰӮжһңжңүйўқеӨ–зҡ„еҚҡеЈ«жҲ–иҖ…еҚҡеЈ«еҗҺеҗҚйўқ,дјҡз»§з»ӯеҸ‘е№ҝе‘ҠжӢӣиҒҳгҖӮ

жңҖеҗҺи°Ҳи°Ҳд»Ҡе№ҙзҡ„ж–Үз« гҖӮд»Ҡе№ҙжҖ»е…ұеҸ‘иЎЁдәҶ 4 зҜҮ journal articles,1 зҜҮ book chapter,1 зҜҮGIScience зҡ„дјҡи®®и®әж–ҮгҖӮе…¶дёӯ book chapter е’ҢдёҖзҜҮжңҹеҲҠжҳҜеҮәиҮӘз¬¬дёҖе№ҙMScеӯҰз”ҹзҡ„йЎ№зӣ®,дјҡи®®жҳҜд»Ҡе№ҙжүӢжңәappж•°жҚ®з ”з©¶зҡ„дёҖдёӘз®ҖеҚ•жұҮжҖ»,иҝҳжңүеҸҰеӨ–2зҜҮжңҹеҲҠжҳҜе…ідәҺдјҳеҢ–е’ҢеҹҺеёӮзғӯеІӣзҡ„еҗҲдҪңж–Үз« гҖӮе”ҜдёҖзҡ„дёҖзҜҮдёҖдҪңжҳҜжҠҠ 2018 е№ҙеҚҡеҗҺжңҹй—ҙеҒҡзҡ„з ”з©¶ж”¶е°ҫдәҶ,д№ҹз®—жҳҜжңүе§Ӣжңүз»ҲгҖӮеј•з”Ёж–№йқўжІЎжңүд»Җд№Ҳзү№еҲ«зҡ„зӘҒз ҙ,иҝҳжҳҜиҖҒж ·еӯҗдёҚжё©дёҚзҒ«гҖӮдёҖзӣҙд»ҘжқҘеҜ№дәҺз§‘з ”зҡ„иҝҪжұӮиҝҳжҳҜеңЁдәҺй•ҝжңҹзҡ„з§‘з ”е…ҙи¶Ј(жңүи¶ЈдҪҶжҳҜеҫҖеҫҖжІЎжңүд»Җд№ҲзӣҙжҺҘзҡ„з”ЁеӨ„)е’Ңе®һйҷ…зҡ„зӨҫдјҡд»·еҖј(жңүз”Ё,е°Өе…¶еңЁзҺ°еңЁзҡ„зӨҫз§‘йўҶеҹҹ),и°ҒзҹҘйҒ“е“ӘеӨ©зғӯзӮ№е°ұиҗҪиҮӘе·ұеӨҙдёҠдәҶе‘ўгҖӮеҪ“з„¶иҝҷдәӣйғҪжҳҜеҹәдәҺиӢұеӣҪд»ҺеҠ©зҗҶж•ҷжҺҲе°ұејҖе§Ӣзҡ„й•ҝжңҹеҗҲеҗҢеҲ¶еәҰеёҰжқҘзҡ„жҹҗз§ҚзЁӢеәҰдёҠзҡ„еӯҰжңҜиҮӘз”ұеҗ§,еҰӮжһңиҰҒиҜ„ tenured иҮӘз„¶жҳҜжңүеҸҰеӨ–дёҖз§ҚзҺ©жі•гҖӮзҺ°еңЁеңЁе®Ўзҡ„ж–Үз« жңүдёӘ 3-4 зҜҮ,еҠ дёҠе’ҢеҺ»е№ҙзҡ„ MSc еҗҢеӯҰдёҖиө·еҒҡзҡ„ 2 зҜҮж·ұеәҰеӯҰд№ еә”з”Ёзҡ„ж–Үз« ,е’ҢдёӯеҝғеҚҡеҗҺдёҖиө·еҒҡзҡ„mobile phone app е’Ң housing sensor enhance survey зҡ„ 3-4 зҜҮж–Үз« ,иҝҳжңүдёҖдәӣйЎәзҗҶжҲҗз« иҰҒеҶҷзҡ„ proposal,жҳҺе№ҙеә”иҜҘиҝҳжҳҜдјҡжңүеҫҲеӨҡеҶҷдҪңд»»еҠЎгҖӮ
