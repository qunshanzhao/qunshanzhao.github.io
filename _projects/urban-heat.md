How do people experience extreme heat — and cold — inside and outside their homes? We combine sensor-enhanced housing surveys, secondary data, and urban climate modelling and instrumentation to understand indoor overheating and thermal comfort in London and Glasgow: from long-term adaptation and acute heat shocks in domestic environments to AI-driven citywide hourly mapping of thermal stress.

See the [publications]({{ '/publications/' | relative_url }}) page for related papers, including work in *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society A* (2025) and *Sustainable Cities and Society* (2026).
